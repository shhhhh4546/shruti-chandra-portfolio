01
HCI Research Co-Design ADHD Mixed Methods

MoneyTalk

A financial management tool co-designed with adults who identify as having ADHD, built across four phases: literature review, Reddit discourse analysis, survey, and five co-design sessions.

The Problem

Financial tools were designed with neurotypical cognitive patterns in mind - assuming users engage consistently, sustain attention through multi-step workflows, and build habits. For many adults with ADHD, these assumptions do not hold. All existing tools were also reactive: they track spending after it occurs.

Process

1 Lit Review → 2 Reddit → 3 Survey → 4 Co-Design
1,668 Reddit posts analyzed across 6 communities
71% of posts tagged Price/Value, the top theme
6.5/7 mean rating for affordable pricing in survey
$30–$100 friction threshold range across 5 participants

"I want this app to be annoying. I need the friction."

P05 · High friction · $30 threshold
vs.

"I don't want to be nagged for small stuff."

P04 · Low friction · $100 threshold

Same population, opposite needs - the fundamental design requirement: the tool must be configurable, not built around a single assumed user.

02
Experiment Pre-Registered Color Vision Figma Plugin

Accessible Figma: Color Filter for Deuteranopia

A pre-registered 2×2 factorial experiment testing whether a custom assistive color filter plugin improves task performance for designers who have deuteranopia, not just those designing for them.

The Problem

Figma's built-in color blindness filters were designed for normal-vision designers to preview how their work looks to color-blind users. No major design tool offers a feature to assist designers who themselves have color vision deficiency.

Design: 2×2 Between-Subjects

Filter OFFFilter ON
DeuteranopiaGroup A · n=10Group B · n=10
Normal visionGroup C · n=10Group D · n=10
−43s Task time reduction for deuteranopic users with filter
−49% Error rate reduction (5.1 → 2.6 errors)
−11 Fewer clicks for deuteranopic users with filter on
n.s. Effect on normal-vision users - no significant difference

Bayesian analysis: BF₁₀ = 32.90 - very strong evidence for the interaction. Accessibility features designed for a specific group do not penalize general users.

Pre-registered Protocol

Stage 1 registered report submitted before data collection. Protocol deviations documented in Section 5.9.

Open Data

All data, analysis files (JASP), and the Stage 1 registered protocol available in the project repository.

03
Lean Six Sigma DMAIC Process Improvement Figma

Improving Figma's Prototyping Workflow

A Lean Six Sigma DMAIC project applying statistical process control, DOE, and waste analysis to reduce UX prototype creation time from 47 minutes to a projected 22.

The Problem

UX designers spent an average of 47.2 minutes creating interactive prototypes - nearly twice the 25-minute target. DPMO of 958,968 (<1 sigma). 60% of creation time was non-value-adding waste. Many designers skipped prototyping entirely, handing static mockups to developers instead.

DMAIC

DDefine - VOC interviews, Project Charter
MMeasure - SPC charts, 1-sample Z-test (p < .0001)
AAnalyze - 2³ DOE (16 runs), waste map, Ishikawa
IImprove - component kit, auto-layout, variables
CControl - SOPs, KPI dashboard, feedback loop
958,968 DPMO before improvement (<1 sigma)
60% of prototype time was non-value-adding waste
−19.5 min Time saved by pre-built component library (DOE)
47→22 min Projected reduction in prototype creation time
